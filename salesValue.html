Qualification Foundation

Ensure prospects meet core criteria before presenting
Plain Language: If a customer is qualified in these areas... we are likely to make a sale.

Strong qualification is the foundation of effective objection handling. Before presenting, confirm the prospect meets our five core criteria:

Budget Allocated

  • $1MM-$10MM available
  • Pre-seed to Series A
  • Mid-market companies
  • Heavily invested startups

Target Sectors

  • Healthcare systems
  • Games & entertainment
  • Defense contractors
  • Related tech verticals

Tech Forward

  • 5-year technology vision
  • Internal training programs
  • R&D investment
  • Cross-industry solutions

Value Aligned

  • Treats customers well
  • Respects employees
  • Commitment to excellence
  • Substance over flashiness

Visionary Leadership

  • Sees competitive advantage
  • Values human insight
  • Strategic thinking
  • Innovation mindset

Presentation Opening

Move past niceties into meaningful discovery
Plain Language: This is roughly what we say to start the conversation.

Opening Framework:

"I heard about [the problem] and we think we can help, but I want to learn more."
"Walk me through [the complex problem]."
"What have you tried before that didn't work?"
"What would success look like for your team?"

Critical: Listen for what really matters to the customer. Use their language and pain points to customize your presentation and handle objections effectively.

The Carrot Pulling Method

Dig deeper than surface needs to uncover core concerns
Plain Language: This is what we want them to say. Not exactly this, but this level based on what we hear and how we respond. This process takes practice and improves over time.

Most salespeople harvest "carrot tops" - easy surface-level needs. To handle objections effectively, you must pull the whole carrot by drilling down to deeper concerns.

ü•ï Surface Level (Carrot Top)

"We need better branding."
Customer talks about logos, colors, website design
Surface need: Visual identity

ü•ï Deeper Level (Middle Carrot)

"What's driving the need for better branding right now?"
"Our customers don't understand what makes us different from competitors"
Deeper need: Market differentiation

ü•ï Root Level (Whole Carrot)

"How is that affecting your team and your customers day-to-day?"
"Our sales team struggles to explain our value, and customers choose cheaper alternatives because they don't see why we're worth more"
Core concern: Revenue impact from poor value communication

Objection Handling: Surface vs. Deep

How qualification depth transforms objection responses
Plain Language: If you qualify well, you have ammo to handle objections. That doesn't always happen, but this is why you qualify. Which of these options is more likely to overcome objections?

Common Objection: "You're more expensive than other options we're considering."

‚ùå Surface Response (Carrot Top)

Sales Rep Says: "Well, you get what you pay for. We deliver high-quality work and our clients are very satisfied."


Why It Fails: Generic response that doesn't address the customer's specific situation. Sounds like every other vendor.

‚úÖ Deep Response (Whole Carrot)

Sales Rep Says: "I understand price is a concern. Earlier you mentioned your sales team struggles to explain your value, and customers choose cheaper alternatives. That's exactly why we focus on human insight in our branding, events, and technical solutions - we help you articulate what makes you worth more, so you can stop competing on price. The cost of losing deals to cheaper competitors is much higher than our fee."


Why It Works: Ties price objection directly to their revenue problem, positioning Ladybug as the solution to their deeper concern.
